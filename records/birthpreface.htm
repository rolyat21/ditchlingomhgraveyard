REGISTER OF BIRTHS

In the 17th century Parish Registers tended to be records of baptisms rather than of births. The General Baptists did. not practise infant baptism. There was a reluctance amongst some Parish Clergy to enter births into their registers even when requested to do so. The result was that in the early days of dissent few records of births were kept other than in family bibles or journals.

As difficulties arose the inevitable consequence was the creation of non-parochial registers of various kinds. The Horsham Meeting began their own Register in 1724, accepting entries from all who so requested including a few entries from Ditchltng. Another such Register was that kept by Dr Williams's Library.

Tha keeping of a Register of Births for the Ditchling Meeting seems to have been first raised in 1785 when it was minuted: "Agreed that a Book be bought to Register Ghildrena Names in when Born - To be done by H Browne "

No action was taken possibly due to Henry Browne being preoccupied with the setting up of a business in Lewes with his partner William Crosskey. Again in 1791 it was minuted: " It is the wish of this Meeting that a Book be bought to register Ghildrens names in - they have therefore appointed Jas Browne to procure the same and execute the business "

James Browne, a younger brother of Henry, was Secretary to the Meeting. Still no action was taken.

In 1803 Abraham Bennett, former Minister of the General Baptist Meeting in Bishopsgata, was appointed Elder. It was he who, in 1811, began the Register. His first entries wore those of his children born some years earlier.

Following the departure of Mr. Bennett to a ministry at Poole arid the brief ministry of William Browne, the Register was taken over by James Browne. He made some amends for his earlier inactivity by gathering and entering details of some earlier births and thereafter keeping the Register with his customary care.

George Withall was appointed Minister in 1852 for by this time the term 'Elder' had been quietly dropped. Thereafter the Register was in his hands though few entries were made.

In 1837 there came into operation the Act of 3 & 4 Vict cap 92 relative to the General Registration of Births, Marriages and Deaths. Amongst the provisions of this Act, all non-parochial registers were to be deposited in the General Register Office. This was duly done and the Ditchling Register of Births is now to be found in the Public Record Office, manuscript RG4 1796.

The Register is a large book. Most of the pages are unused. On the flyleaf is pasted a Return evidently made before it, along with the Deaths Register, was deposited. In the Return, George Vithall certifies, inter alia: 'I am the Minister of the General Baptist Chapel Ditchling The principal acting trustees are John Wood, Jesse Kense-fct, Edward Turner, all of Ditchling The Register of Burials at the Burial Ground of the General Baptist Chapel, Cuckfield has been kept by Mr James Walder of Long Acre House, Cuckfield'

A copy of the Register was made before the original was deposited. This copy has survived though in a somewhat mutilated cond.ition. No subsequent Register was kept. The new Act rendered it unnecessary.

In making the transcription of the Register of Births some simplification of format has been adopted together with some abbreviations, viz:

R Registered by
Dr WL Registered at Dr Williams's Library
AB Abraham Bennett(Protestant Dissenting Minster)
WB William Browne
GW George Withall
JB James Browne

The Register entries are numbered 1 to 62 but with a gap between 16 and 25. The last entry was in 1852. The nntries appear to cover only a small, proportion of the births of children to members of the Church.
