REGISTER OF DEATHS AND BURIALS

This has been compiled from a number of sources. Firstly there is the Register covering the period 1821 to 1837 which was deposited in the General Register Office along with the Register of Births under the 1837 Act and now held by the Public Record Office, Document No KG4 1797. The 68 entries in this Register have been transcribed using a simplified format as was done for the Register of Births.

Secondly there is an additional Register held in the Meeting House archives (MS DL6). It begins with a copy of the 68 entries of the original Register and then carries on, the last entry being dated February 1950.

This second Register bears the Imprint of many hands, both in the original entries and in corrections or additions added by later hands. The title of the Register changes as time goes on. The original flyleaf reads: 'Register of Ditchling General Baptist Chapel Burials' . The outer cover, in a later hand reads: 'Register of Chapel Burials' . A later brown paper dust cover bears the title: 'Ditchling Free Christian Church Burials'

There are a number of gaps in the Register suggesting that it was lost or was inaccessable to those officiating at funerals for some periods. Some of the gaps were noted in a list in the handwriting of Miss Emily Cooke entitled 'Burials not. entered in Register - On Stones' (lMS DL99) and citing 24 names. For ease of reference tills information has been added to the Register as transcribed, in date order and marked *. No attempt has been made to add details of Deaths and Burials before 1821 when the original Register was started. Where such information is available, it will be found in the Register of Graves and Monumental Inscriptions or the Supplementary List.
