GRAVES & MONUMENTAL INSCRIPTIONS

The Register which follows has been compiled from four principal sources:
a) A plan of -the Meeting House and Burial Grounds prepared at the time of the creation of the .... Trust Deed together with a partly completed Schedule of those persons buried in the Old Burial Ground MS DL23
b) A pencil sketch, undated, by an unknown hand and entitled 'The Floor of the Chapel before being boarded' and giving some details of the grave stones exposed at that time MS BL48
c) A record of gravestone inscriptions compiled by Miss Emily Cooke (1852-1921) from personal observation and using an earlier record of inscriptions made by Colonel F W T Attree. This record was loaned to the Transcriber by her kinswoman Miss Jessie Read. It is referred to hereafter as MS DL107
d) A visual check of all surviving gravestones a-nd memorial tablets by the Transcriber and members of his family

The numbering system used to identify graves in the Old Burial Ground is that found in the 1854 plan. Elsewhere the numbering system is that of the Transcriber. A plan at the end of this volume locates the position of each individual grave by number.

The Burial Register, up to 1837 and when referring to the New Burial Ground, generally locates the grave by the distance in feet/inches from the East and South walls. vftien referring to the latter, it should be remembered that, in 1849 the South wall was moved some 12 feet or so right up to tha boundary of the property
